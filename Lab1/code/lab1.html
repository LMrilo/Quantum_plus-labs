From Linear Algebra to Quantum Reality

Time: 45 min Prerequisites: QC03, QC04

In this laboratory, you will move from the mathematical abstraction of vectors to actual quantum manipulation. The goal is to develop an intuition for how gates affect the state of a qubit, visually (via Composer) and statistically (via Measurement).

Part A: Visual Intuition (Composer)

Tool: Open IBM Quantum Composer in a separate tab.
Exercise 1: The "Quantum Switch" (X Gate) GATE X
Scenario: Imagine you have a classical light switch that is OFF (0). To turn it ON (1), you apply a force. In quantum computing, the "switch" is the qubit initialized at $|0\rangle$, and the "force" is the X Gate.
Theory [QC04]: The X gate rotates the state vector by 180° around the X-axis. $$ X|0\rangle = |1\rangle $$ This is a "bit flip" operation.
Instructions:
  1. Verify the qubit line $q_0$ is empty (State $|0\rangle$).
  2. Drag the X Gate (Cyan) onto the line.
  3. Check Probability: The blue bar jumps to 100% on "1".
IBM Composer View (Q-Sphere):
Look at the sphere. You should see a single dot at the Bottom (South Pole).
  • Position: South (means State 1).
  • Color: Blue.
    Why Blue? Because the state is simply $|1\rangle$ (Phase 0). No complex phase has been introduced yet.
Theoretical Bloch Sphere

Vector points DOWN ($|1\rangle$).

Exercise 2: Entering Superposition (H Gate) GATE H
Scenario: In the classical world, a coin is either Heads or Tails. Here, we spin the coin on a table. While spinning, it is effectively both Heads and Tails. The H Gate "spins the coin", putting the qubit into a perfect superposition.
Theory [QC03]: The Hadamard gate creates the state $|+\rangle = \frac{|0\rangle + |1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$. Both coefficients are positive real numbers.
Instructions:
  1. Delete the X gate. Drag the H Gate (Blue) onto the line.
  2. Check Probability: You see 50% on "0" and 50% on "1". This represents pure randomness.
IBM Composer View (Q-Sphere):
The vector points to the Equator, but the Q-Sphere shows this as Two Dots.
  • One dot at North ($|0\rangle$).
  • One dot at South ($|1\rangle$).
  • Color: Both are Blue.
Interpretation: The Blue color confirms that both components ($|0\rangle$ and $|1\rangle$) have the same positive phase.
Theoretical Bloch Sphere

Vector points to X-Axis ($|+\rangle$).

Exercise 3: The Hidden Phase Flip (Z Gate) GATE Z
Scenario: This is the hardest concept for beginners. Imagine the spinning coin from Exercise 2. If you look at it from above, it looks identical whether it spins clockwise or counter-clockwise. Similarly, the Z gate changes the "spin direction" (Phase) without changing the "shape" (Probability).
Theory [QC04]: The Z gate creates the state $|-\rangle = \frac{|0\rangle - |1\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$. The minus sign is on the $|1\rangle$ component. This corresponds to a phase of $\pi$ ($e^{i\pi} = -1$).
Instructions:
  1. Keep the H gate. Place a Z Gate (Green) immediately after it.
  2. Check Probability: Notice it is STILL 50/50. The "shadow" hasn't changed.
  3. Look at the Q-Sphere: This is the only place you can see the change.
IBM Composer View (Q-Sphere):
You still see two dots, but look at the colors:
  • Top Dot ($|0\rangle$): Stays Blue (Phase 0).
  • Bottom Dot ($|1\rangle$): Turns RED.
Why Red? As per our legend, Red represents Phase $\pi$ (the minus sign). The system is now in the state $|0\rangle - |1\rangle$.
Theoretical Bloch Sphere

Vector points to NEGATIVE X-Axis ($|-\rangle$).
